Creating Competitive Advantage through Culture

High-performance organizations (teams) aren’t lucky accidents. They're developed and led by competent, confident and consistent high-performance leaders. These leaders understand that a powerful strategy and structure are necessary yet insufficient without an appropriate / healthy culture.

Leadership is taking people places they wouldn’t go on their own. Leadership is getting people to achieve and do what they wouldn’t achieve and do on their own. The keys to effective leadership are the practice of self-awareness, empathy, trust, and continuous learning, and impactful communication. Effective leaders are fueled by an inner drive that’s visible from the demonstration of their authentic commitment. That commitment gets channeled through their skillful utilization of candor, courage, communication, and collaboration.

Leaders are positive influencers. Leaders trade in trust. They know trust is the primary currency for effective relationships. Trust is enhanced through connection, inspiration, personal and organizational alignment. Trust overcomes fear, defensiveness, cynicism, and apathy -- the factors that destroy hope, meaningful action, and empowered execution.

Leaders model the way, encourage the heart and inspire people to leave their comfort zones for something potentially better, bigger and bolder. Leaders create the conditions for people to find a way or make a way to deliver results that make the difference.

Gregg Baron, CMC

Gregg Baron, CMC, is an author and certified management consultant*. His expertise is in the areas of utilizing the art and science of positive influence, the leadership practices for leading change, and executive facilitation. Some of his clients include Delta Airlines, Honeywell, Royal Caribbean Cruises, Verizon, Carnival Cruise Line, CIGNA, KPMG, Celebrity Cruises, Cisco Systems, Sandals Beaches Resorts, Comcast, JP Morgan Chase, Marriott Vacation Club, IBM, Bank of America.

His first book, 250 Ways to Increase Your Top and Bottom Lines, was published by Dartnell. He co-authored the book Leadership without Excuses: How to Create Accountability and High Performance and it was published by McGraw-Hill. Gregg has also been published in a variety of professional journals on topics such as leadership and influence practices and customer experience. He is one of fifty contributing authors to the American Marketing Association’s Marketing Encyclopedia: Issues and Trends Shaping the Future.

In addition to his research and writing, Gregg has hosted his own video show, Successful Business Relationships, on a major airline. He founded Success Sciences, headquartered in Tampa.

*CMC® (Certified Management Consultant) is the certification mark awarded by the Institute of Management Consultants USA and represents meeting the highest global standards and ethical canons of the profession. Fewer than 1% of all consultants have achieved this level of excellence.
